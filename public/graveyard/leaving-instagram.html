01.

the perfect grid
was a performance.

i've always hated performing — feeling like i had to prove something with a perfectly curated grid.

but it was a good practice. it taught me to see how my work fits together — the themes that surmise.

i'm taking that with me. leaving the rest.

02.

moving everything
back home.

i've always dreamed of bringing all my data to my own homepage — and joining the "indie web."

a place, not a platform. no algorithm. no ads. no harvest.

the site is the gallery. the site is the art.

03.

i miss the
old internet.

i want to go back to a time when webpages weren't —

weren't surveillance.
weren't engagement engines.
weren't shaped like everyone else's.

i miss handmade pages. tiled backgrounds. guestbooks. weird little spaces built by one person.
